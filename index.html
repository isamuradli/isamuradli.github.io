I'm a Computer Science PhD candidate at the Illinois Institute of Technology, advised by Dr. Xian-He Sun and Dr. Anthony Kougkas, working on high-performance computing — specifically parallel and distributed processing, I/O optimization, and bringing GPU-accelerated compute to standalone HPC applications to address the memory wall altogether. I'm currently a PhD intern at NVIDIA, mentored by Quincey Koziol. Before my PhD, I was an Associate Data Scientist at Exelon in Washington, DC. I'm a member of the Gnosis Research Center at Illinois Tech.

Outside of work, I enjoy playing volleyball and tennis, and spending time with history and literature books.
